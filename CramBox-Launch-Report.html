1. Executive Summary

Verdict: CramBox is ready to ship for a soft launch on June 1, 2026.
The core product is functional, secure, and tested. Several enhancements are recommended before scaling, but none block the initial launch to a small group of students.

CramBox is a mobile-first web application that helps Zimbabwean O-Level students prepare for ZIMSEC exams. It runs entirely in the browser — no app store, no download, no data required after initial load. Students activate with a unique code, then access structured lessons, practice questions, speed rounds, panic mode, and an infinite drill engine across 12 math topic generators.

2. Product Overview

2.1 What CramBox Is

A single-file progressive web app (127KB HTML) that teaches ZIMSEC O-Level Mathematics through interactive lessons, practice tests, and infinite drill. It works offline after first load, runs on any phone with a browser, and costs zero data to use day-to-day.

6. Scaling Strategy (June — December 2026)

Phase 1: Seed (June 1-30) — 50 students

  • Distribution: Personal WhatsApp contacts, family networks
  • Payment: Manual EcoCash collection, codes sent via WhatsApp DM
  • Goal: Validate that students actually use it and find value
  • Metrics to track: Activation rate, lessons completed, drill sessions per day, WhatsApp shares
  • Action: Ask every student for feedback. Fix what's broken.

Phase 2: Word of Mouth (July) — 200 students

  • Distribution: WhatsApp group marketing, student referrals
  • Viral mechanic: WhatsApp score sharing already built in — students share scores, friends ask "what's CramBox?"
  • Referral incentive: "Refer 3 friends, get a free subject unlock" (implement simple referral tracking)
  • Action: Add 2-3 more subjects (English, Science, Commerce) to unlock the Full Bundle tier

Phase 3: Schools (August-September) — 500-1,000 students

  • Distribution: Approach teachers and school administrators directly
  • Pitch: "Your students can revise from their phones for $1 each. No data needed. No app download. Works on any phone."
  • School license: Generate bulk codes, deliver to teacher, teacher distributes
  • Action: Create a 1-page school flyer. Visit 5-10 schools in person.
  • Key timing: August/September is prime revision season before Oct/Nov exams

Phase 4: Exam Season (October-November) — 2,000+ students

  • Distribution: Facebook/Instagram ads targeting Zimbabwean parents and students
  • Panic mode marketing: "Exams in 2 weeks? CramBox has you covered."
  • Price increase: Raise to $5 during peak exam season (urgency pricing)
  • Action: Integrate Paynow for automated EcoCash payments + instant code delivery

Phase 5: Expand (2027) — 10,000+ students

  • Add A-Level subjects (expand TAM)
  • Add other African exam boards (Cambridge, CAPS South Africa, KNEC Kenya)
  • Build a proper landing page and payment flow
  • Consider a WhatsApp bot for automated code delivery
  • Explore partnerships with telcos for zero-rated data access

8. Risks & Mitigations

RiskSeverityMitigation
Code sharing — students share activation codes MEDIUM Device fingerprint lock already prevents multi-device use. One code = one device.
Content piracy — someone copies the HTML file MEDIUM Activation gate blocks access without valid code. Content is in-memory only after activation. At scale, speed of iteration > piracy risk.
Google Sheets limits — backend can't handle scale MEDIUM Apps Script handles ~20K requests/day free. At 1,000 students, activation is a one-time event. Migrate to Supabase/Firebase only if hitting limits.
Content accuracy — wrong answers in questions HIGH All 12 drill generators use forward-verification (generate answer, then solve independently to confirm). Structured lessons are manually authored. Have a teacher review content.
localStorage wiped — student loses all progress MEDIUM Currently no cloud backup. Phase 2: add progress sync to Google Sheets or simple backend.
Payment collection — manual EcoCash is tedious MEDIUM Manageable at 50-200 students. Integrate Paynow (Zimbabwe payment gateway) by Phase 4.
Only Maths at launch — limits appeal LOW Maths is the #1 O-Level subject. Launch with strength, expand subjects based on demand.

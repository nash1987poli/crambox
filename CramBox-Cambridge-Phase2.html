01 — WHY THIS, WHY NOWThe Cambridge Gap

The ZIMSEC campaign is built around a sharp positioning signal: "made for OUR exam." That signal is what makes parents click. If we add "and also Cambridge" to the headline, we kill it — the message becomes generic, the trust drops, the campaign weakens.

But Cambridge IGCSE is a real market we'd be wrong to ignore long-term:

  • Used by ~15–20% of Zimbabwean O-Level students (private schools — Petra, Hellenic, Watershed, Eaglesvale, Heritage, Hillcrest, etc.).
  • Same families pay $80–150 per Cambridge exam paper — $5–6 for prep is rounding error.
  • Used across at least 12 African countries — same product, 10× the addressable market.
  • October/November AND May/June sessions — twice the launch windows per year.

Why separate URLs (not /cambridge subdomain)

Parents trust specificity. cambridge-prep.vercel.app says "built for Cambridge" the same way zimsec-prep.vercel.app says "built for ZIMSEC." A unified URL forces the user to navigate to their syllabus — that's a friction point that costs conversions. Two URLs = two clean positioning surfaces.

Code architecture (technical)

Switch the content track via a build-time env var or URL parameter. The DATA object in index.html becomes DATA.zimsec and DATA.cambridge — same shape, different questions. The accent CSS variable swaps based on which build is loaded. EXAM_DATES becomes a SKU-specific config.

05 — WHY $6 WORKS FOR CAMBRIDGEAnchoring Reframed

The pricing simulator (in CramBox-Campaign-Interactive.html) showed $6 is too high for ZIMSEC because it crashes the viral coefficient. For Cambridge, three things flip:

  1. Different anchor. ZIMSEC anchors against $30 tutors. Cambridge anchors against $100 exam fees. $6 vanishes.
  2. Different income bracket. Cambridge parents are paying ~$300/term in school fees. $6 isn't an impulse decision — it's a no-decision.
  3. Different distribution mechanic. Cambridge students share less on Status (privacy norms differ) but parents talk in tighter networks (church, business associations, school WhatsApp groups). Word travels through fewer, higher-leverage nodes.

Tested in the simulator: at $6 against a $100 anchor, conversion stays in the 22–28% range (vs ~12% at $6 against the $30 anchor). The number drops but doesn't collapse.

07 — RISKS & UNKNOWNSPre-Launch Open Questions

RISK 01

Cambridge content is harder to author

0580 Extended covers more advanced topics (matrices, vectors, function transformations, deeper trig). Authoring time per topic could be 2× ZIMSEC. Mitigation: launch with Core 0580 first (simpler), add Extended in v2.

RISK 02

Cambridge students share less publicly

Private school culture is more reserved. The Share My Score loop that powers ZIMSEC virality may underperform here. Mitigation: add a "send to parent" share option that targets the actual decision-maker.

RISK 03

Payment friction in regional markets

EcoCash works in Zim only. SA needs PayFast/PayShap, Kenya needs M-Pesa, Nigeria needs Flutterwave/Paystack. Each integration is a separate vetting and KYC process. Mitigation: launch Cambridge with Stripe (cards) globally first, add local rails as country volume justifies.

RISK 04

Cannibalisation

Some Cambridge students may have bought ZIMSEC by mistake. They'd get angry — refund / migrate? Mitigation: on the ZIMSEC landing page, add a clear "Are you sitting Cambridge IGCSE? → cambridge-prep.vercel.app" link at the bottom.

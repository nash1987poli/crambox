What CramBox is

Built for how Zimbabwean students actually study.

A self-contained study app that helps students pass O-Level Mathematics (ZIMSEC 4004) — on a shared phone, on a tight budget, often without reliable data.
Positioning: "Study all month for the price of one data bundle — and never spend data again." CramBox is the high-intensity cram weapon for the weeks before the exam, not another year-long course.

The problem it solves

  • Data is expensive — streaming tutorials and PDFs burn money. CramBox works offline after one load.
  • Tutors are costly — $30+/hour. CramBox is $5 once.
  • Textbooks are passive — CramBox is interactive, gamified, focused on what comes up.
  • Students study in groups sharing one phone — CramBox embraces it with Team Spar.

The AI approach — baked in, not live. AI writes the exam-style questions and worked solutions up front; they ship inside the app file. So the "AI coach" runs offline, at zero per-student cost, with no exposed API key — protecting both the offline promise and the margins. A live online "Challenge Mode" is reserved as a future premium tier.

Under the hood

  • Architecture: one self-contained HTML app on Vercel; activation via Google Apps Script + Sheet — lean, cheap, resilient.
  • Reliability: automated test suites cover the question banks, scoring, and game logic.
  • Activation: one-time code, device-locked; Squad codes unlock multiple devices.

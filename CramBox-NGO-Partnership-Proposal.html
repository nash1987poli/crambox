1. The Problem: Africa's Exam Preparation Crisis

100 million children and youth aged 6-18 in Sub-Saharan Africa are excluded from formal learning. Of those in school, only 15% achieve minimum learning outcomes. The teacher-to-student ratio averages 1:40.

Across Sub-Saharan Africa, students preparing for national examinations face compounding barriers:

  • Textbooks are scarce and expensive — $10-30 per subject, often shared among 5+ students
  • Private tutoring is out of reach — $20-50/month in countries where median income is under $200
  • Mobile data is prohibitively expensive — streaming educational videos on YouTube costs $1-3/day in data alone, often 5-10% of daily income
  • Existing EdTech solutions are Western-centric — Khan Academy, Quizlet, and others don't cover ZIMSEC, WAEC, KNEC, or CAPS curricula
  • The digital divide persists — only 46% mobile internet penetration, with rural access under 10%

The result: millions of capable students fail exams not because they lack ability, but because they lack access to quality revision materials in the format they actually use — their phones.

2. The Solution: CramBox

CramBox is a mobile-first, offline-capable exam preparation platform built specifically for African national curricula. It runs entirely in the browser — no app store download, no ongoing data costs, no expensive device required. One 127KB page load, then it works forever.

SDG 4: Quality Education

CramBox directly addresses Target 4.1 (ensure all youth complete quality secondary education) and Target 4.4 (increase youth with relevant skills). SDG 5: Gender Equality

In many African contexts, girls face disproportionate barriers to tutoring access — safety concerns with travel, cultural restrictions on after-school activities, and family financial priorities favoring boys' education. CramBox eliminates these barriers: students study at home, on their own time, on a shared family device.

5. Partnership Model

We propose a "Build Once, Deploy Everywhere" partnership where the NGO funds curriculum development, localization, and distribution for target countries — while Curious Inc handles all product development, hosting, and technical operations. Students receive CramBox for free or at heavily subsidized rates.

5.1 What the NGO Provides

  • Funding for curriculum development, content experts, and localization
  • Distribution access to schools, teacher networks, and community organizations
  • Local partnerships with education ministries and exam boards for content validation
  • Impact measurement — pre/post exam performance data, usage analytics
  • Credibility — NGO brand association accelerates school adoption

5.2 What Curious Inc Provides

  • The CramBox platform — proven, deployed, security-hardened, code-reviewed
  • Product development — new subjects, new exam boards, new features
  • Technical operations — hosting, activation system, monitoring
  • AI-powered content pipeline — rapid curriculum development using Claude AI

  • Free access (NGO-funded) or heavily subsidized ($0.50-1.00 per student)
  • Curriculum-aligned content for their specific national exam
  • Unlimited practice via procedurally generated drill questions
  • Zero ongoing data costs after initial page load
  • Study tool on the device they already own
